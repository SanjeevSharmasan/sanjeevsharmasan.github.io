Leveraging Generative AI in Finance Operations: Practical Steps for 2026

Published on November 12, 2025 | By Sanjeev Sharma | 8 min read

Key takeaways

  • Start small: identify a high-value, low-risk process to pilot LLMs (e.g., query resolution, report drafting).
  • Design guardrails: data governance, PII filtering, and human-in-the-loop review are essential.
  • Measure impact: track time saved, error reduction, and stakeholder satisfaction.
  • Upskill teams: blend technical skills with domain knowledge and change management.

Why generative AI matters for finance

Generative AI and large language models (LLMs) are changing how finance teams work. From faster month-end narratives to automated vendor communications, LLMs let teams focus on judgement and relationships rather than repetitive text and triage work. But the value only appears when adoption is pragmatic, governed, and aligned to measurable outcomes.

Practical 6-step approach to get started

  1. Identify a pilot: choose a specific use case with clear metrics (e.g., reduce invoice query resolution time by 50%).
  2. Scope data access: determine what data the model needs and ensure sensitive data is masked or excluded.
  3. Choose tooling: pick a managed LLM or API (Claude, others) with enterprise controls and an audit trail.
  4. Build guardrails: put automated checks, human review for decisions, and explainability where needed.
  5. Measure and iterate: collect KPIs (time, accuracy, NPS) and iterate weekly during the pilot.
  6. Scale thoughtfully: grow to adjacent processes after governance and ROI are proven.

Governance and risk

For finance teams, the primary concerns are data privacy, regulatory compliance, and accuracy. Implement these controls from day one:

  • PII discovery and redaction before sending data to an LLM.
  • Role-based access and logging for all model interactions.
  • Human-in-the-loop for final outputs that affect financial statements or legal obligations.
  • Regular model audits and a rollback plan.
