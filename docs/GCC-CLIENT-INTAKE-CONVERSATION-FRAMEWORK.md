# GCC Client Intake & Conversation Framework

## SECTION 1: CLIENT PROFILE ANALYSIS TEMPLATE

### 1.1 Pre-Call Research (Before Discovery Call)

**When prospect books a call:**
1. Review their LinkedIn profile
2. Take notes on:
   - Current title & company
   - Career trajectory (stable, jumpy, steady growth?)
   - GCC mention? Related experience?
   - Connection points (mutual connections, shared interests)
3. Note their profile headline - What are they looking for?

## SECTION 2: DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT & FRAMEWORK

**Duration**: 20-30 minutes (Initial Assessment - paid or free, your choice)

### 2.1 Opening (2 minutes)

```
"Hi [Name]! Great to meet you. Thanks for scheduling this. 
I'm Sanjeev, and I've been guiding professionals like you through 
successful GCC transitions. 

In the next 20 minutes, I want to understand your background, 
where you want to go, and whether GCC is the right move. 
Then I'll share if I think I can help you get there. Sound good?"
```

**Vibe**: Confident, empathetic, solution-focused

---

### 2.2 Their Story (5-7 minutes)

**Questions to Ask:**

1. **"Tell me about your current role and background. What field are you in?"**
   - *Listen for*: Years of experience, functional expertise, stability
   - *Red flag*: Completely unrelated to GCC/finance/ops

2. **"What's prompting you to explore GCC now?"**
   - *Listen for*: Specific trigger (boredom, growth, company decision, market shift)
   - *Red flag*: Vague answer, no real motivation

3. **"When are you thinking about making this move? Next 3 months, 6 months, a year?"**
   - *Listen for*: Realistic timeline
   - *Red flag*: "I don't know" or unrealistic urgency

4. **"Do you have any experience in GCC, shared services, or process optimization?"**
   - *Listen for*: Relevant exposure (even if not formal title)
   - *Red flag*: No exposure + unrealistic timeline (needs more prep)

5. **"What specifically attracts you to GCC?"**
   - *Listen for*: Career growth, stability, pay, skill fit
   - *Red flag*: "I heard it pays well" only (short-term thinking)

### 2.3 Challenges & Concerns (4-5 minutes)

**Questions to Ask:**

6. **"What's your biggest concern or worry about making this transition?"**
   - Common answers: "I don't have GCC experience", "Too old", "Will I like it?", "Interview prep"
   - *Your response*: "That's common. We'll address this in our program"

7. **"What do you think you'll need to successfully prepare for GCC interviews?"**
   - *Listen for*: Self-awareness - do they know what's needed?
   - *Red flag*: No idea = need more education phase

8. **"Are there any constraints we should know about?"** 
   - *Listen for*: Job search timeline, financial needs, visa/location, family considerations
   - *Red flag*: Visa sponsorship needed = more complex

### 2.4 Expectations & Fit (3-4 minutes)

**Questions to Ask:**

9. **"Ideally, what would success look like for you 3 months from now?"**
   - *Listen for*: Specific goal - "GCC offer", "Interview confidence", "Company list"
   - *Red flag*: Undefined success criteria

10. **"How committed are you to this? What would you be willing to invest - time and money?"**
    - *Listen for*: "2 hours/week" + "Yes, I have budget for coaching"
    - *Red flag*: "I need to think about it" or "Can't spend money"

### 2.5 Closing & Next Steps (1-2 minutes)

**If Good Fit (90% confidence):**
```
"[Name], based on our conversation, I think you're a strong candidate 
for GCC transition. You have [specific strength], and I can absolutely 
help you navigate this.

Here's what I recommend:
- 12-week intensive program: $2,000 (12 sessions)
  OR
- Try 4-session trial: $700 to see if we vibe

Sessions are 1-hour, weekly, and we'll focus on interview prep, 
targeting companies, and building your GCC roadmap.

Does one of these work for you?"
```

**If Uncertain Fit (50-75% confidence):**
```
"[Name], I see potential, but I want to be honest with you. 
You'll need [specific preparation] before we dive deep. 

What I'd recommend:
- Let's start with the 4-session trial ($700)
- We'll assess gaps in weeks 1-2
- Then decide if full 12-week is right

Fair?"
```

**If Poor Fit (<50% confidence):**
```
"[Name], I appreciate the conversation. To be transparent, 
I don't think you're quite ready for GCC transition right now because [specific reason].

Here's what I'd recommend instead:
[Alternative - gain X experience, take Y course, wait Z months]

If you want to reconnect in [timeframe], I'm happy to. 
And feel free to reach out to [alternative resource]."
```

**Never force a bad fit - your reputation matters more than one client.**

## SECTION 3: PERSONALITY & COMMUNICATION ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

### 3.1 During Call - Observe & Note

**Personality Indicators:**
- [ ] Punctual? (Reliability)
- [ ] Camera on? (Professionalism)
- [ ] Prepared? (Took notes? Asked questions?)
- [ ] Listening? (Let you finish or interrupts?)
- [ ] Tone? (Confident, anxious, curious, rushed?)
- [ ] Energy? (Engaged or disconnected?)

**Example Note-Taking:**
```
"Sarah - Punctual, prepared with questions. Slightly anxious about age (50s) 
but very eager. Good listening skills. Tone: Professional but warm. 
Energy: High engagement. Pain: Worried about competing with younger candidates."
```

### 3.2 Communication Preferences (Ask)**

**"How do you prefer to communicate between sessions?"**
- Email? Slack? WhatsApp? Phone?
- Frequency? Weekly updates or as-needed?
- Response time expectations?

## SECTION 6: "WHY GCC?" CONVERSATION

### 6.1 Common Reasons (Validate & Probe)

**"Better Work-Life Balance"**
- Validate: "That's true, GCC tends to have structured schedules"
- Probe: "Are you burned out now? What specifically needs to change?"
- Reality check: "GCC can still be demanding during peak seasons. You okay with that?"

**"Better Pay/Stability"**
- Validate: "GCC roles are often stable with good compensation"
- Probe: "What's the financial goal? Is it about security or growth?"
- Reality check: "You may take a short-term dip (6 months) before ramping up"

**"Want to Move into Tech/Modern Ops"**
- Validate: "GCC is absolutely on the tech transformation front"
- Probe: "What tech interests you most - automation, AI, analytics?"
- Reality check: "GCC roles vary - some tech-heavy, some not. Let's find the right fit"

**"Growth/Career Progression"**
- Validate: "GCC is a clear progression path to director level"
- Probe: "Do you see yourself leading teams or staying individual contributor?"
- Reality check: "We'll map your specific progression path in week 1"

**"Change of Pace/New Challenge"**
- Validate: "That's smart - career stagnation is real"
- Probe: "What specifically excites you about a new challenge?"
- Reality check: "Every new role has a learning curve - you ready for that investment?"

### 6.2 Red Flags in Motivation

🚩 **"I just need a job"** → Likely to struggle in GCC culture
🚩 **"My friend/spouse got into GCC"** → External motivation, may lose focus
🚩 **"I heard it's easy"** → Misaligned expectations
🚩 **"I'm desperate to leave my current job"** → Running FROM, not running TO

**Coaching needed**: Help them clarify the actual reason + rebuild motivation

## SECTION 7: TESTIMONIAL COLLECTION FRAMEWORK

### 7.1 When to Ask

**Best timing**: After client gets offer or completes program
**Don't ask**: Too early or if client unhappy

### 7.2 Testimonial Request Template

```
"[Client name], I'm so proud of your progress. 
I'd love to share your story to help other professionals 
considering the GCC transition.

Would you be open to a short testimonial? 
It would help me help more people like you.

Here's what would be helpful to hear:
1. What was your biggest challenge before we started?
2. How did our program help you overcome it?
3. What's one thing you'd tell someone considering GCC?
4. Would you recommend working with me? Why?

Take your time - 2-3 sentences is perfect!"
```

### 7.3 Follow-Up Success Story

After offer:
```
"Congratulations on the offer, [Name]! 
This is HUGE. I'd love to feature your story on my site/LinkedIn.

Can I share:
- Your previous role
- Timeline to offer (how long did it take?)
- One challenge you overcame
- One piece of advice for others

I'll send you the draft before posting for approval."
```

## SECTION 8: SURVEY QUESTIONS FOR ONGOING CLIENTS

### 8.1 Post-Session Feedback (Quick - 2 min)

**After each session:**
```
On a scale of 1-10:
1. How useful was today's session? __
2. Do you understand the homework? __
3. Any topics you want to revisit next week? ___
4. One word to describe how you're feeling about GCC prep: ___
```

### 8.2 Mid-Program Pulse Check (Week 6)

```
1. How confident do you feel about GCC interviews now? (1-10) __
2. What's been most valuable about our program so far?
3. What topic do you still need help with?
4. Are you applying to jobs yet? How's it going?
5. Any changes to your timeline or goals?
```

### 8.3 Post-Program Assessment (Week 12)

```
1. Did the program meet your expectations? How?
2. What was the biggest breakthrough for you?
3. What's your next step (apply, interview, negotiate)?
4. Would you recommend me to others? (Yes/No/Maybe)
5. Any feedback to improve the program?
```

### 8.4 Referral/Testimonial Ask (Post-Offer)

```
1. What was your biggest fear before starting?
2. How did you overcome it? (Your specific homework/mindset shift)
3. What was the turning point - when did it click?
4. What's one thing you'd tell someone starting this journey?
5. How likely are you to refer a friend? (1-10)
   - If 8+: "Can I get a referral?" 
   - If <8: "What would make it a 10?"
```

## SECTION 9: COMMON CONVERSATION PATTERNS

### 9.1 The Hesitant Prospect

**They say**: "I'm not sure if I can afford this..."
**You say**: "I get that. This is an investment in your future. Let's look at ROI:
- Average GCC salary increase: $15,000-25,000/year
- Our program: $2,000
- That's a 7-12x return in year 1 alone
- Plus: You get [specific benefit]

Let's do the 4-session trial ($700) and see the value first?"

### 9.2 The Overconfident Prospect

**They say**: "I don't think I need much prep. I'll just apply."
**You say**: "I respect the confidence. Let me ask - have you interviewed for GCC before?"
- If no: "Here's what I see - GCC has a specific interview style [explain]. 
  Most people who interview without prep [specific mistake]. 
  What if we did a free mock interview first?"
- If yes: "What happened? How did it go?"

### 9.3 The Anxious Prospect

**They say**: "I'm worried I'm too old/not experienced enough..."
**You say**: "That's the [most common worry/I hear that often]. 
Here's the reality: [specific example from portfolio]. 
We'll address this specific concern starting day 1.
Our first homework: Identify [specific strength they have].
I promise you're not as behind as you think."

### 9.4 The Unmotivated Prospect

**They say**: "I'm just exploring options..."
**You say**: "I appreciate the honesty. Question for you: 
If you got the right GCC offer tomorrow - one that paid X, had Y benefits, 
and was at company Z - would you take it? 
[Listen to response]
That answer tells us if you're ready to invest in preparation or if you want to explore more first."

## SECTION 10: DISQUALIFICATION SCRIPT

**When to say no:**

```
"[Name], I want to be respectful of both our time. 
After our conversation, I don't think I'm the right fit for you right now because:

[Pick one]:
- You're not ready for GCC transition yet (need X first)
- Your timeline is unrealistic (Y months is not enough for Z preparation)
- You're exploring vs. committed (I work best with committed clients)
- Your goals don't align with my program (I specialize in [X], you need [Y])

Here's what I'd recommend instead:
[Alternative path / resource / when to revisit]

I wish you the best, and feel free to reach out if circumstances change."
```

**This builds trust**: People respect experts who know their boundaries.
