```

- 12:00-1:00 PM: Lunch + 1 mentoring session
- 5:00-6:00 PM: 1 mentoring session (evening slot for working professionals)
```

- Batch administrative tasks (2-3 hour blocks)
- Use templates to reduce prep time per session

## PART 3: CLIENT ACQUISITION & QUALIFICATION

### 3.1 Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

**Target Persona: "The Ready Transitioner"**
- Working professional (5-10 years experience)
- Considering GCC transition (next 3-6 months)
- Willing to invest $300-500+ for guidance
- Actively learning (reads, takes courses, engages)
- Typically: Finance/HR/Operations/IT background

**Avoid/Be Cautious**
- "Just browsing" energy
- Expect free consulting
- No commitment/follow-up
- Unrealistic expectations

### 3.2 Discovery Call Framework

**Goal**: Qualify fit + understand their specific needs

**Structure (15-20 min)**:
1. Warm opening (2 min) - rapport
2. Their story (5 min) - current role, why GCC, timeline
3. Challenges (3 min) - what worries them most?
4. Expectations (3 min) - what do they hope to achieve?
5. Next steps (2 min) - offer packages, book if fit

**Success Indicators**:
- [ ] They articulated specific pain points
- [ ] Timeline is realistic (3-12 months)
- [ ] Budget not an issue
- [ ] Willing to commit to weekly/bi-weekly sessions

## FAQ: CONSULTING + JOB SEARCH BALANCE

**Q: Won't consulting distract from job search?**
A: If limited to 8 hours/week and properly scheduled, it enhances your profile (real experience, testimonials, network).

**Q: How do I explain this to employers?**
A: Frame as "Side mentoring practice while exploring opportunities" - shows initiative, expertise, network.

**Q: What if I get a job offer?**
A: You can either close the consulting practice or offer a transition plan to clients (handoff, group sessions, async content).

**Q: How to handle rate increases?**
A: After 10 clients: Increase to $250-300/session. New clients at higher rate, existing clients at negotiated rate or grandfathered.
