# GCC Mentoring Session Templates

## SECTION 1: SESSION STRUCTURE OVERVIEW

### 1.1 12-Week Program Timeline

| Week | Focus | Goals | Key Activities |
|------|-------|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | Assessment | Understand background + create roadmap | Profile review, gap analysis, SWOT |
| 2 | Foundation | GCC basics | Role deep-dive, company research, market overview |
| 3 | Self-Positioning | Build compelling narrative | STAR stories, elevator pitch, LinkedIn optimization |
| 4 | Technical Foundation | Core GCC knowledge | Best practices, process optimization, case studies |
| 5 | Company Research | Target list + insider knowledge | Industry analysis, company culture, interview formats |
| 6 | Interview Strategy | Common questions + frameworks | Q&A prep, story bank, objection handling |
| 7 | Mock Interview 1 | First full interview simulation | 60-min mock + detailed feedback |
| 8 | Interview Debrief + Advanced Topics | Learn + prepare for harder questions | Q&A refinement, complex scenarios, leadership questions |
| 9 | Mock Interview 2 | Second simulation with improvements | 60-min mock + coaching |
| 10 | Advanced GCC Topics | Specialized knowledge | Automation, AI in GCC, emerging trends, client examples |
| 11 | Mock Interview 3 | Final simulation | Full interview + offer negotiation prep |
| 12 | Closing + Launch | Finalize + begin applications | Offer strategy, ongoing support, referral ask |

## SECTION 2: SESSION 1 - INITIAL ASSESSMENT (90 minutes)

### 2.1 Agenda

```
0:00-0:05   Welcome + Agenda
0:05-0:20   Their Background Story
0:20-0:35   GCC Knowledge Assessment
0:35-0:50   SWOT Analysis + Gap Identification
0:50-1:20   Create 12-Week Roadmap
1:20-1:30   Homework + Next Steps
```

### 2.2 Detailed Script

#### **Opening (5 min)**

```
"Welcome! I'm excited to work with you over the next 12 weeks. 
Here's what today is about:

1. I'll understand your background deeply
2. We'll assess where you are with GCC knowledge
3. I'll identify specific gaps
4. We'll create YOUR personalized roadmap

By the end, you'll know exactly what to focus on to land a GCC offer.

Any questions before we dive in?"
```

#### **Their Background Story (15 min)**

**Questions:**
1. "Walk me through your career. What roles led you here?"
2. "What's your strongest skill/area of expertise?"
3. "Tell me about a time you optimized a process or led change"
4. "What's your proudest professional achievement?"
5. "Why NOW for GCC? What's triggering this move?"

**Your role**: Listen actively, take notes, find STAR stories to weave into interviews

**Probe deeper**: 
- If vague: "Can you give me a specific example?"
- If strong story: "That's gold - we'll use this. What made you successful there?"

#### **GCC Knowledge Assessment (15 min)**

**Questions (score their answers 1-10):**

1. "What do you understand about what GCC actually does?"
   - 8-10: They understand core functions
   - 5-7: Partial understanding
   - <5: Need education

2. "Name 3-5 GCC companies you'd want to work for"
   - 8-10: Specific companies, knows their services
   - 5-7: Can name companies but limited detail
   - <5: Doesn't know where to apply

3. "Tell me about a GCC role you're interested in. What would you do?"
   - 8-10: Specific, understands day-to-day
   - 5-7: General understanding
   - <5: Vague idea

4. "What do you think the interview process looks like?"
   - 8-10: Knows typical format (behavioral + technical)
   - 5-7: Basic idea
   - <5: No clue

5. "What's your biggest knowledge gap right now?"
   - Self-aware: Good starting point
   - Unaware: You'll help them discover

**Your response to answers**:
- Great answer: "Excellent - you've done your homework"
- Partial answer: "Good start - here's what I'd add..."
- Weak answer: "That's common - we'll deep-dive in Week 2"

#### **SWOT Analysis + Gap Identification (15 min)**

**Create a table together:**

| Strength | Weakness | Opportunity | Threat |
|----------|----------|-------------|--------|
| [e.g., 10 yrs in finance] | [e.g., No GCC interview experience] | [e.g., Tech transformation in GCC] | [e.g., Age bias] |
| [e.g., Process improvement skills] | [e.g., Limited company knowledge] | [e.g., Digital transformation] | [e.g., Competition from Gen Z] |

**Then ask:**
- "Which weakness worries you most?" → Becomes focus area
- "Which opportunity excites you most?" → Becomes motivation
- "How can we turn threats into strengths?" → Mindset shift

#### **Create 12-Week Roadmap (30 min)**

**Structure the conversation:**

```
"Based on what I've learned, here's how I see the next 12 weeks:

Weeks 1-4: Foundation & Positioning
- You'll become a GCC expert (our knowledge hub + deep dives)
- You'll have 3-4 powerful STAR stories ready
- Your LinkedIn will scream 'GCC ready'

Weeks 5-6: Target Strategy
- We'll identify your target companies (15-20 realistic options)
- You'll understand each company's GCC model
- You'll start networking/applying

Weeks 7-9: Interview Mastery
- 3 full mock interviews with feedback
- Answer framework for every common question
- Practice until it's second nature

Weeks 10-12: Closing
- Negotiate offer
- Navigate final interviews
- Launch into your new role

Your homework between sessions:
- Week 1: [Specific task]
- Week 2: [Specific task]
- Apply: [Number] of jobs/week

Does this roadmap feel right to you?"
```

**Adjust if needed**: 
- "I see you're anxious about interviews - let's add mock interview earlier"
- "You're already networking well - let's speed up the technical prep"

#### **Homework + Next Steps (10 min)**

**Assign Week 1 Homework:**

```
"Your homework for next week:
1. Read [article/guide on your website] - 30 min
2. Make a list of 10 companies interested in - 30 min
3. Write down 3 times you improved a process (bullet points) - 20 min
4. Answer this: 'In one year, I'll be [successful GCC scenario]. 
   Here's why I'll get there: [reasons]' - 15 min

Total: 90 minutes of prep work
This is what separates successful candidates from casual explorers.

Any questions?"
```

**Confirm next session:**
- "Our next session is [date/time] - same time next week?"
- "Between now and then, feel free to email me questions"

**Close strong:**
```
"I'm genuinely excited about your potential. 
You've got [specific strength], and with focused prep, 
you'll absolutely nail the GCC transition. 
Let's make this happen together."
```

## SECTION 3: WEEKLY SESSION TEMPLATE (60 minutes)

### 3.1 Standard Session Flow

```
0:00-0:03   Check-in + Vibe Setting
0:03-0:10   Homework Review
0:10-0:45   Main Topic Deep-Dive
0:45-0:57   Apply to Real Scenario
0:57-1:00   Homework Assignment + Closing
```

### 3.2 Detailed Weekly Session Script

#### **Check-in (3 min)**

```
"Hey! How's it going? How was your week?"

[Listen - they might say:]
- "Great, I'm making progress" → Stay positive
- "Tough week, didn't get much done" → Troubleshoot + support
- "Anxious about interviews" → Tackle anxiety head-on

Adjust your energy to match theirs, then say:

"Before we dive deep, I want to check:
- Any questions from last week's homework?
- Anything urgent we should address today?
- Are we on track for your timeline?

If yes to anything - let's tackle it first."
```

#### **Homework Review (7 min)**

**Questions to ask:**

1. "Tell me about your homework. What did you learn?"
2. "What was hardest about it?"
3. "What surprised you?"
4. "How will you use this in interviews?"

**Scoring their homework:**
- 🟢 Complete + thoughtful: "This is exactly what I needed to see"
- 🟡 Partial + effort: "Good start - here's what I'd add"
- 🔴 Not done: "What got in the way? Let's problem-solve"

**Never shame them**: 
- "I hear you - this is new territory" 
- "Let's do a mini version right now"
- "What support would help you complete this?"

#### **Main Topic Deep-Dive (35 min)**

**Example: Week 3 - STAR Story Development**

```
"This week we're mastering STAR stories. 
Why? Because 80% of GCC interviews are behavioral questions.

Here's the framework:
- Situation: The context (company, role, challenge)
- Task: What you were responsible for
- Action: Specific steps you took (focus here!)
- Result: Quantifiable outcome

Let's build 3-4 stories from your background that GCC interviewers want to hear.

Story #1: Process Improvement
- Situation: [From their homework]
- Task: [What they owned]
- Action: [What they specifically did - not team, not manager - THEM]
- Result: [Impact - time saved? Cost reduced? Quality improved?]

Tell me the situation?"
```

**Build together**: You ask probing questions, they share stories, you refine

**Typical breakdown**:
- 5 min: Explain framework
- 20 min: Build 1-2 stories together
- 10 min: Polish language + delivery

#### **Apply to Real Scenario (12 min)**

```
"Let's practice. Here's a real GCC interview question:

'Tell me about a time you had to implement a process change 
that faced resistance from the team. How did you handle it?'

Using the STAR framework we just discussed, 
walk me through your answer. I'll stop you if I hear something unclear."

[They answer - listen for:]
- Do they use S-T-A-R?
- Is the action specific to them?
- Is the result quantifiable?
- Did they take ownership?

Then feedback:
"You know what I liked? [Specific positive]. 
One thing to strengthen: [Specific coaching]. 
Let me show you how to say it..."
```

#### **Homework Assignment (2 min)**

```
"Next week's homework:
1. Refine your STAR stories - write them out in bullet points
2. Record yourself telling one story (1-2 min) - practice delivery
3. Send me the recording - I'll give feedback
4. Read [article] about X topic we'll cover next week

Time needed: 90 minutes

This work compounds - you're building an arsenal of stories."
```

#### **Closing (1 min)**

```
"Great session today. I'm impressed with your progress.
You're [specific observation - "asking better questions" / "more confident" / "clearer thinking"].

Next week we'll [preview] - and you're ready for it.

Any final questions? Otherwise, get that homework done and I'll see you next week!"
```

## SECTION 4: MOCK INTERVIEW TEMPLATE (90 minutes)

### 4.1 Pre-Mock Setup

**Before the session:**
1. Send them interview guidelines
   - "This is NOT a real interview - I'll stop for feedback"
   - "I'll throw tough questions at you - it's deliberate"
   - "We're practicing, not judging"
2. Ask them to have: Pen/paper for notes, calm space
3. Brief them on format: 45 min interview + 45 min feedback

### 4.2 Mock Interview Flow

#### **Opening (3 min - Fake Interviewer Mode)**

```
[Professional tone, not your usual coaching voice]

"Hi [Name], thanks for joining! 
I'm [fake title], hiring manager for [Company]. 
We're excited to learn more about you.

We'll talk for about 45 minutes - I'll ask questions about your background, 
experience, and GCC fit. Feel free to ask questions at the end.

Ready to get started?"
```

#### **Interview Questions (40 min)**

**Mix of behavioral + technical:**

1. **Opening**: "Walk me through your background. Why GCC?"
   - Listen for: Clarity, preparation, genuine interest
   - If weak: "Why specifically GCC?" (probe)

2. **Behavioral - Process Improvement**: 
   "Tell me about a time you improved an inefficient process. 
   How did you measure success?"
   - Listen for: STAR framework, quantifiable results, ownership

3. **Behavioral - Challenge/Conflict**: 
   "Describe a situation where you disagreed with your manager/team. 
   How did you handle it?"
   - Listen for: Respect, problem-solving, maturity

4. **Technical - GCC Knowledge**: 
   "What's your understanding of [O2C / P2P / AP / HR BPO]?"
   - Listen for: Understanding, curiosity, recent learning

5. **Situational**: 
   "You're managing 3 processes, all have issues. 
   Which do you tackle first and why?"
   - Listen for: Prioritization, business thinking

6. **Hypothetical**: 
   "A process you own has a critical error that costs the client. 
   Your director is upset. What do you do?"
   - Listen for: Accountability, solution-focus, maturity

7. **Closing**: 
   "What questions do you have for us?"
   - Listen for: Preparation, thoughtful questions (not "When do I start?")

#### **Interview Feedback Debrief (45 min)**

**Transition out of fake interviewer mode:**

```
"Okay, switching back to coach mode now. 
Let's break down what just happened. 
First - how do YOU think you did?"

[They self-assess - this tells you about self-awareness]
```

**Feedback structure - Sandwich Model:**

**1. What Went Well (5-10 min)**
```
"Here's what I noticed that's interview-ready:
✓ Your energy was calm and confident
✓ Question [X] - you used STAR framework perfectly
✓ [Specific story] - that was compelling
✓ You asked great closing questions

This is what interviewers want to see. Keep this up."
```

**2. What Needs Work (20-30 min)**
```
"Here's where we can strengthen:

Question [Y] - Your answer was too long/vague/off-topic
→ Here's what happened: [Analysis]
→ Better approach: [Specific coaching]
→ Let's practice it right now

Question [Z] - I noticed you hesitated
→ That tells me you're not fully confident in your answer
→ Here's how to handle this..."
```

**3. Specific Coaching (15-20 min)**

For each weak area, teach & practice:

```
"Let me show you a better way to answer [Question].

The interviewer was asking about [actual intent].
Your answer addressed [what you said] - close, but not quite.

A better structure is:
1. [First element]
2. [Second element]
3. [Third element]

Let's try again - question is: [Repeat question]"

[They practice - you give feedback in real-time]

"That's better. Next time, make sure you [specific coaching point]"
```

**4. Action Items (5 min)**

```
"Here's what I want you to work on before next week:

1. Practice [Question Y] 5 times - record yourself
2. Rewrite your answer to [Question Z] - send me draft
3. Clarify your knowledge on [Topic] - read [resource]
4. Work on your pacing - you rushed through [specific answer]

Total prep time: 3-4 hours
I know that's a lot, but you're close to being truly ready."
```

## SECTION 5: SPECIALIZED TOPIC DEEP-DIVES

1. **What is GCC? (5 min)**

   - Metrics & KPIs they care about
   - Growth opportunities

5. **Interactive Q&A (10 min)**
   - "What questions do you have?"
   - Address their specific curiosities

**Homework**: 
- Watch [company GCC overview video]
- List 3 ways GCC is evolving

### 5.2 Interview Question Framework Session (Week 6)

**Cover 15-20 common GCC questions:**

**Category 1: Background & Fit (20%)**
- "Why GCC?"
- "What attracts you to shared services?"
- "Why our company specifically?"

**Your framework**: 
```
"For ANY 'Why GCC' question, your answer has 3 parts:
1. Personal reason (What pulled you toward this?)
2. Professional reason (What aligns with your goals?)
3. Company-specific reason (Why THIS company?)

Let me show you with an example..."
```

**Category 2: Process & Optimization (30%)**
- "Tell me about a process you improved"
- "How would you handle a high-error process?"
- "Walk me through your approach to process mapping"

**Your framework**: 
```
"These are testing your THINKING PROCESS, not just your stories.

The interviewer wants to see:
1. Do you understand root cause analysis?
2. Can you measure impact?
3. Do you consider stakeholder impact?

Here's the framework..."
```

**Category 3: Challenges & Behavior (30%)**
- "Tell me about a time you failed"
- "How do you handle pressure?"
- "Conflict with a colleague - how'd you resolve?"

**Your framework**: 
```
"They're testing maturity and EQ here.
The best answers show:
1. Self-awareness (you owned your part)
2. Solution-focus (here's what I did about it)
3. Learning (here's what I'd do differently)

Notice - it's NOT about blame..."
```

**Category 4: GCC Knowledge (20%)**
- "What do you know about [Process]?"
- "How would you prioritize [X], [Y], [Z]?"
- "What are the latest trends in GCC?"

**Your framework**: 
```
"For GCC-specific questions, you need:
1. Baseline knowledge (you did your research)
2. Thoughtful perspective (you can think, not just recite)
3. Curiosity (you ask smart follow-ups)

Here's how to show all three..."
```

## SECTION 6: ADVANCED TOPICS SESSION (Week 10)

### 6.1 RPA & Automation in GCC

```
"GCC is being transformed by automation. 
You need to know about RPA (Robotic Process Automation).

What it is: Software bots that do repetitive tasks
Common in GCC: Invoice processing, data entry, reconciliation

You should know:
- RPA is not replacing people - it's changing roles
- Jobs are evolving toward analysis + strategy
- Smart candidates are embracing automation, not fearing it

Example interview question:
'We're automating 40% of our O2C process. How does that affect your role?'

Weak answer: "That's scary for my job"
Strong answer: "Great - that frees the team to focus on [strategic work]. 
I'm interested in learning RPA tools and how to optimize processes further."
```

### 6.2 AI & Predictive Analytics

```
"AI is the next frontier in GCC. You should have a basic understanding.

Common applications:
- Predictive analytics for collections
- Fraud detection in invoicing
- Intelligent document processing
- Chatbots for employee services

You don't need to be an AI expert, but show:
1. Awareness (you know it exists)
2. Curiosity (you're learning)
3. Openness (excited about innovation, not threatened)

How this might come up in interviews:
'What excites you about the future of GCC?'

Answer framework:
'I'm fascinated by [specific technology]. 
I've been reading about [use case]. 
I'd love to be part of a team that's implementing these innovations.'
```

### 6.3 Business Acumen

```
"GCC leaders think about business, not just processes.

Key concepts to understand:
- Margin = How much money the company makes
- Throughput = How much work can we do with current resources
- Cost per unit = How much does each transaction cost?
- SLA = Service level agreement (quality/speed standards)
- P&L = Profit and Loss statement

For interviews:
'Walk me through how a GCC function contributes to company profitability'

Answer framework:
'GCC manages [X] transactions for [Y] cost per transaction. 
By optimizing efficiency, we improve [Z], which directly impacts 
company bottom line. That's why metrics matter.'
```

## SECTION 7: CLOSING SESSION (Week 12)

### 7.1 Agenda

```
0:00-0:10   Celebrate Progress
0:10-0:20   Application Strategy
0:20-0:30   Offer Negotiation Prep
0:30-0:50   Advanced Interview Scenarios
0:50-1:00   Ongoing Support + Referral Ask
```

### 7.2 Script

#### **Celebrate Progress (10 min)**

```
"Before we look forward, let's celebrate how far you've come.

12 weeks ago, you were [specific state they were in].
Now, you:
- [Specific improvement 1]
- [Specific improvement 2]  
- [Specific improvement 3]

You're genuinely interview-ready. This is real."
```

#### **Application Strategy (10 min)**

```
"Here's my recommendation for applying:

Target companies (rank by fit):
[Your list of 15-20 companies]

Application strategy:
- Tier 1 (dream companies): Apply directly + network internally
- Tier 2 (strong fit): Apply through recruiter + personal outreach
- Tier 3 (solid options): Apply directly, less networking

Timeline: 
- Weeks 1-2: Apply to 20 companies
- Weeks 3-4: Follow up + start interviews
- Month 2: Negotiate offers

Your job now: Apply + let interviews come to you.
My job: Help you prepare for each one.

Any questions about the strategy?"
```

#### **Offer Negotiation Prep (10 min)**

```
"When (not if!) you get an offer, here's how to negotiate:

First rule: Never accept the first offer without thinking.
Always say: 'Thank you - I'm excited. Can I review and get back to you in 24 hours?'

Then think about:
- Salary (is it market rate? Room to negotiate 5-10%?)
- Sign-on bonus
- Remote work flexibility
- Timeline to promotion
- Learning budget

Best negotiation is BEFORE they offer:
At end of interview, ask: 'What would someone in this role earn?'
This sets expectations early.

We'll prep specific negotiation scenarios as offers come."
```

#### **Advanced Scenarios (20 min)**

```
"Let's prep for curveballs:

Scenario 1: 'You don't have GCC experience. Why should we hire you?'
Your answer: [Practice]

Scenario 2: 'You're overqualified. We're worried you'll leave.'
Your answer: [Practice]

Scenario 3: 'Someone else has GCC experience. Why you?'
Your answer: [Practice]

Let's practice each one..."
```

#### **Ongoing Support (5 min)**

```
"After today, here's what I'm offering:

Free support (included):
- Email access to ask quick questions
- Help on final interview prep before offers

Premium support (additional fee):
- 2-3 sessions during interview phase for specific interview prep
- Negotiation coaching when you get an offer

Most people get 2-3 interviews in first month, then offer within 6-8 weeks.
You're now part of my GCC family - we support each other."
```

#### **Referral Ask (2 min)**

```
"Finally - if you found value in this program:

1. Can I get your testimonial? (2-3 sentences about your experience)
2. Know someone else considering GCC? Please refer them - 
   I'll give you $50 credit if they sign up
3. Connect with me on LinkedIn + share my program with your network

You've invested in yourself. Help others do the same."
```
