## SECTION 3: END-TO-END CONVERSATION PLAN

### 3.1 Complete Call Script (30 minutes)

#### **Pre-Call (5 min before)**
- You: Check tech, have notes ready, calm energy
- Send 1 minute early: "Looking forward to our call in a few mins!"

#### **Opening (2 minutes)**

```
"Hi Poornima! Thanks so much for scheduling this. I'm Sanjeev.

Before we jump in, quick context - I run a GCC career mentoring program. 
I've been helping professionals like you navigate the transition into GCC roles.

I also built a knowledge hub that's helped 1,000+ people prepare for GCC interviews.

In the next 30 minutes, I want to:
1. Understand YOUR story and goals
2. See if GCC is the right move for you right now
3. Share whether I think I can help

Sound good?"
```

**Vibe**: Warm, professional, genuinely interested (not transactional)

#### **Their Story - PART 1: Background (5 minutes)**

```
"Tell me about your background. 
What have you been doing career-wise, and what led you to explore GCC?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Years of experience
- Relevant skills (process improvement, operations, finance, tech)
- Current role satisfaction
- Trigger for GCC transition

**Follow-up questions** (based on their answer):
- "In your role at [Company], what was your biggest win?"
- "What's been most rewarding about [type of work]?"
- "What's prompting the change now?"

**Your response pattern:**
- If relevant experience: "That's gold - you've got [specific skill] that GCC values"
- If unrelated: "That's valuable for a different reason - let me explain..."

---

#### **GCC Fit Assessment - PART 2: Motivation (5 minutes)**

```
"So you're interested in GCC. Help me understand - what's appealing about it?"
```

**Listen for their top 1-3 reasons.**

**Then probe each one:**

**If they say "Better work-life balance":**
```
"That makes sense. GCC does typically have more structured hours than [their current field].

Quick reality check though - during month-end/quarter-end, 
GCC teams do work overtime. You okay with that trade-off?

And more importantly - sounds like you might be burned out now? 
Tell me about that."
```

**If they say "Better pay/stability":**
```
"Absolutely - GCC roles have strong compensation and stability.

Fair warning though - you might take a small dip in year 1 
while you prove yourself. Then it ramps up. Good?"
```

**If they say "Growth opportunity":**
```
"Great mindset. GCC has a clear path to director level, 
which is what you want?

Walk me through - see yourself leading teams 
or staying more technical as you grow?"
```

**If they say "Change of pace/new challenge":**
```
"Smart to recognize when you need a reset. 
What specifically would feel fresh to you?"
```

**If answer is vague or unconvincing:**
```
"I appreciate the honesty. Sounds like you might still be exploring?

That's fine - but let me be real with you: 
my program works best with people who are genuinely committed to GCC. 

Are you at that point, or are you still weighing options?"
```

## SECTION 8: QUICK ASSESSMENT CHECKLIST (For Each New Client)

**Complete within 24 hours of first meeting:**

### 8.1 Green Light Checklist

**Good fit = Most boxes checked**

- [ ] Has realistic timeline (3-12 months)
- [ ] Shows genuine GCC interest (not just job hunting)
- [ ] Willing to invest time + money
- [ ] Asks thoughtful questions (shows engagement)
- [ ] Communicates clearly
- [ ] Receptive to feedback/coachable
- [ ] Has relevant (or adjacent) experience OR strong learning aptitude
- [ ] Follows up promptly (sends forms, shows up on time)

**Action**: Enroll in program confidently

### 8.2 Yellow Light Checklist

**Uncertain fit = 4-6 boxes checked**

- [ ] Timeline is tight but possible
- [ ] Motivation is mixed (some clarity, some exploration)
- [ ] Budget concerns but willing to invest
- [ ] Questions are generic
- [ ] Communication is professional but not warm
- [ ] Defensive about feedback
- [ ] Limited experience but willing to learn
- [ ] Takes time to respond

**Action**: Start with 4-week trial, not full 12-week

### 8.3 Red Light Checklist

**Poor fit = <4 boxes checked**

- [ ] Timeline is unrealistic (<3 months or 2+ years)
- [ ] Just exploring, no real commitment
- [ ] Can't/won't invest financially
- [ ] No questions - passive listening
- [ ] Communication is difficult (unclear, dismissive)
- [ ] Defensive or resistant to feedback
- [ ] No relevant experience + not willing to learn
- [ ] Non-responsive

**Action**: Politely decline, refer elsewhere, or set future touchpoint
