## 1. Role Overview & Strategic Context {#role-overview}

### Understanding the Shared Services Model

In shared services environments (especially for MNCs and Big 4 consulting firms), O2C/AR/Credit/Collection functions serve as centralized hubs supporting multiple business units, geographies, and legal entities. Your role bridges:

- **Retained Finance Function:** Strategic decision-making, policy setting, governance
- **Outsourcing Partner:** Operational execution, transaction processing, day-to-day activities
- **Business Units:** Customer relationships, revenue recognition, cash management
- **Finance Operations:** Month-end close coordination, reporting, financial controls

### Key Differentiators for Director vs Senior Manager

| Aspect | Senior Manager | Director |
|--------|----------------|----------|
| **Scope** | Regional or functional (AR or Collections) | Global multi-functional (O2C + AR + Credit + Collections) |
| **Span** | 50-100 FTE (mixed) | 200-500+ FTE across multiple locations |
| **Budget** | $2-5M operations budget | $15-40M+ P&L accountability |
| **Strategy** | Tactical execution, process optimization | Strategic transformation, partnership governance |
| **Stakeholders** | Functional teams, internal clients | C-suite, board committees, partner CEOs |
| **Decisions** | Process, team, technology implementation | Service model, location strategy, M&A integration |

### Question 2: Describe your experience with outsourcing transitions. Walk us through a situation where the transition was difficult—what happened, and how did you fix it?

**STAR Answer (Director Level - Real Scenario):**

**Situation:**
During an AR/O2C outsourcing transition to a Tier-1 BPO partner (600+ transactions daily, $500M+ AR portfolio), the go-live was problematic:
- Within 30 days: Exception rates jumped from 12% to 32% (expected 10%)
- Customer master data wasn't properly migrated → 18% of invoices had customer matching issues
- Payment routing logic wasn't configured correctly → 22% of payments went to wrong accounts
- The outsourcing partner's training was inadequate → team struggled with company-specific processes
- Our retained team was defensive ("I told you outsourcing wouldn't work")
- Business units were frustrated with billing delays
- Vendor escalations increased 300%

**Task:**
I was brought in to stabilize the relationship within 90 days, prevent deal termination (worth $3.5M annually), and transition to steady-state operations.

**Action:**

**Phase 1: Crisis Stabilization (Days 1-15)**
1. **Root Cause Analysis:**
   - Flew to partner site in India; spent 2 weeks on-the-ground
   - Interviewed partner team, reviewed transaction logs, identified specific failure points
   - Key findings: Data migration was incomplete (customer IDs, payment routing rules not updated); partner's understanding of invoice matching logic was insufficient

2. **Immediate Fixes:**
   - Implemented temporary manual review layer: All exceptions reviewed within 4 hours (vs. 24+ hours), escalated for immediate action
   - Corrected customer master data: 85% of mismatches resolved within 1 week
   - Reconfigured payment routing logic with partner; provided detailed documentation
   - Established daily war room (CFO, Partner VP Operations, My team) to triage critical issues

3. **Communication & Confidence Rebuild:**
   - Sent daily email to CFO with status: Exceptions down x%, collections restored y%, timeline to stabilization
   - Met with business units weekly: Explained root causes, timeline to resolution, new processes
   - Acknowledged that "we learned lessons—the transition should have been phased; we'll do better next time"

**Phase 2: Process Stabilization (Weeks 3-8)**
1. **Knowledge Transfer & Training:**
   - Invested $150K in intensive on-site training (partner's team + my staff for 3 weeks)
   - Documented all company-specific processes: Customer master logic, credit policies, collection strategies, payment routing
   - Created partner team accountability: Performance bonuses for achieving SLAs

2. **Process Simplification:**
   - Streamlined customer master data entry (reduced from 45 fields to 25 critical fields)
   - Simplified invoice matching rules (removed manual overrides → enabled automation)
   - Automated payment routing to eliminate manual decision-making

3. **Governance & Monitoring:**
   - Deployed real-time dashboards showing: Exception rate, SLA compliance, customer escalations, payment accuracy
   - Set up automated alerts: If exception rate exceeds 18%, escalate to VP level
   - Weekly review of metrics with partner; monthly business reviews with stake holders

**Phase 3: Steady State & Expansion (Months 3-6)**
1. **Performance Improvements:**
   - Exception rates fell to 8% (better than baseline 12%)
   - Payment accuracy improved to 98% (target: 96%)
   - Customer escalations decreased 85%
   - Collections efficiency improved 25%

2. **Stakeholder Confidence:**
   - Presented stabilization results to CFO, board committee
   - Business units saw improved service levels
   - Partner earned "Trusted Partner" status

3. **Strategic Conversation:**
   - With stabilized foundation, I negotiated expanded scope with partner: "Let's add Collections process; you've proven capability"
   - Structured incentive-based relationship: "Achieve 96% SLA and quality targets → get 2% revenue bonus; miss targets → 2% penalty"
   - This shifted dynamic from "we're watching you" to "we're investing in your success"

**Result:**
- **Exception Rate:** 32% → 8% (75% reduction) within 90 days
- **Payment Accuracy:** 78% → 98% within 120 days
- **Customer Escalations:** Down 85%; vendor satisfaction recovered
- **Deal Status:** Moved from "at risk" to "thriving"; expanded scope by 40%
- **Cost Savings:** Original target $3.5M achieved + additional $800K through expanded scope
- **Organizational Learning:** Documented lessons learned; improved future transition playbook

**How to Communicate:**
*"I've managed multiple outsourcing transitions—not all smooth. This particular transition taught me critical lessons: you need robust data quality upfront, you must invest in training, and you have to rebuild trust through transparency and quick wins. My role was crisis management, problem-solving, and rebuilding partnership dynamics. Within 90 days, we moved from deal-termination risk to trusted partnership. That's the kind of leader I am—I solve hard problems, I'm comfortable with ambiguity, and I focus on outcomes."*

### Question 3: Shared services environments require balancing standardization with regional compliance. How do you navigate this tension?

**STAR Answer:**

**Situation:**
At a multinational organization with shared services in Dublin (Europe), Costa Rica (Americas), and India (APAC), I managed O2C operations with 60+ currencies, 15+ tax regimes, and diverse customer payment preferences. The tension: Global standardization for efficiency vs. regional compliance requirements and business practices.

For example:
- **Germany:** Requires specific invoice format (UStG regulations), payment within 14 days (standard business practice)
- **UK:** Different tax reporting requirements post-Brexit
- **Japan:** Customers prefer bank transfer with invoice copy; relationship-based credit terms
- **Brazil:** Localization requirements for invoicing; high interest rates affect DSO strategy
- **US:** Highly competitive; aggressive discounting required for top customers

**Task:**
Design a global O2C operating model that enables standardization (cost efficiency, quality, scalability) while accommodating regional requirements.

**Action:**

1. **"Core + Local" Framework:**
   - **Core (100% standardized):** Master data governance, invoice format template, dunning rules logic, payment routing algorithm
   - **Local (configurable):** Tax/compliance variations, customer payment preferences, credit decision criteria, collection intensity
   - This allows 85% standardized processes with 15% flexibility

2. **Compliance Audit by Region:**
   - Engaged local tax, legal, and compliance teams: "What's required by regulation vs. practice?"
   - Documented: Invoice format requirements, payment timing expectations, tax filing requirements, audit trail needs
   - Mapped to process steps: Where flexibility needed, where standardization possible

3. **Governance Structure:**
   - **Global O2C Council (Quarterly):** All regional finance heads + shared services leadership + compliance/tax
   - **Regional O2C Committees (Monthly):** Regional operations teams + business stakeholders
   - Clear escalation: If regional manager identifies conflict between global standard and local requirement → escalate to council

4. **Specific Examples of Balancing:**
   - **Invoicing:** Global template with mandatory fields (invoice number, date, amount, tax ID) + regional variations (local language, tax requirements, payment instructions)
   - **Credit Policy:** Global framework (credit scoring, approval limits) + regional modifications (industry norms, relationship-based decisions for key customers)
   - **Collections:** Global strategy (dunning sequence, contact frequency) + regional calibration (payment behavior norms, holiday calendars, relationship importance)
   - **Reporting:** Unified global chart of accounts + regional drill-down for compliance reporting

5. **Change Management & Buy-in:**
   - When proposing standardization, involved business units: "Here's what we're standardizing. Here's why (cost, quality, scale). Here's how we maintain compliance. What's your concern?"
   - Addressed concerns openly: "You're right—this might require an interim step. Let's figure out how."
   - Showed respect for regional expertise: "You know your market; help us design a process that works locally AND scales globally"

6. **Technology Enablement:**
   - Invested in configurable systems (Autobank, SAP): Could configure different logic by region without recoding
   - Reduced need for manual workarounds
   - Enabled faster deployment to new regions

**Result:**
- **Standardization Rate:** 85% of processes standardized; 15% locally configured
- **Cost Efficiency:** 22% reduction in shared services headcount vs. previous model
- **Compliance:** 100% on-time regulatory filings; zero audit findings on regional requirements
- **Speed to Market:** New region (e.g., Mexico) deployed in 4 weeks vs. 12 weeks historically
- **Quality:** 96% first-pass accuracy across all regions; uniform SLA compliance
- **Stakeholder Satisfaction:** Business units felt respected; regional teams felt empowered

**How to Communicate:**
*"Balancing global standardization with regional needs is a core challenge in shared services. My philosophy: Build a framework that's clear about what's non-negotiable (governance, compliance, cost) and flexible about how to achieve it (local variations). I engage stakeholders to understand their constraints, I involve compliance/tax in design, and I use technology to enable flexibility without sacrificing efficiency. The result: We move fast, we stay compliant, and business units support us because they feel heard."*

### Question 4: Tell us about your experience with major ERP migrations (SAP ECC → S4/HANA, JDE → SAP). What were the biggest challenges?

**STAR Answer:**

**Situation:**
At a $5B organization, we had legacy ERP landscape: 4 independent SAP ECC instances (EMEA, APAC, Americas, Manufacturing) + JDE for specific business units. This created:
- Duplicate master data (3 customer master systems)
- No integrated view of A/R or O2C
- High maintenance cost ($2.5M annually)
- Inability to standardize processes globally
- Slow innovation (old technology; limited vendor support path)

**Task:**
Lead the consolidation to unified SAP S4/HANA platform supporting global O2C/AR operations; achieve cost reduction, standardization, and enhanced capability.

**Action:**

**Phase 1: Assessment & Strategy (Month 1-3)**
1. **Current State Analysis:**
   - Mapped all 4 SAP instances: customer base, transaction volume, custom configurations
   - Analyzed data quality: 15% duplicate customers, 8% incomplete customer master data
   - Identified custom code: 400+ Z-reports and customizations (60% of code non-standard)
   - Assessed team skills: Need training on new platform

2. **Future State Design:**
   - Consolidated operating model: Single customer master, unified GL account structure, standard O2C processes
   - Phased approach: Region 1 (EMEA - 2 ECC instances) → Region 2 (APAC) → Region 3 (Americas) → JDE sunset
   - Estimated effort: 36 months, $12M investment, 120-person team (internal + consultant support)

**Phase 2: Data Preparation (Month 4-8)**
1. **Master Data Cleansing:**
   - De-duplication of customers (identified 8,000 duplicate records across systems)
   - Validation and completion of customer master (payment terms, credit limits, tax IDs)
   - Historical data migration strategy: Migrate open items; archive closed items

2. **Custom Code Analysis:**
   - Reviewed all 400 custom programs: 45% obsolete, 30% could be standard S/4 functionality, 25% required custom retention
   - Designed approach: Eliminate obsolete code, replace with standard, retain only essential custom

3. **Process Standardization:**
   - Used SAP implementation as opportunity to standardize O2C processes (rather than re-implementing legacy processes)
   - Updated SOPs for new system; trained process owners

**Phase 3: Region 1 Cutover (Months 9-14 - EMEA)**
1. **Pre-Cutover:**
   - Intensive user training: 150 EMEA users trained on new system
   - Mock cutover: Rehearsed cutover process; identified 3 critical issues before go-live
   - Final data load and validation

2. **Go-Live Strategy:**
   - Weekend cutover (Friday afternoon → Monday morning)
   - Parallel run for 2 weeks: Old system + new system running simultaneously; validate equivalence
   - Real-time support team on-site + offshore

3. **Cutover Challenges & Resolution:**
   - **Issue 1:** Cash application logic not fully configured → 15% of payments went to suspense account
     - Resolution: Identified within 4 hours; deployed hotfix in 2 hours; processed suspended payments manually
   - **Issue 2:** Customer master consolidation revealed data integrity issues
     - Resolution: Used S4's validation tools to identify & correct issues; prevented downstream problems
   - **Issue 3:** Users initially frustrated with new UI/processes
     - Resolution: Provided on-site support desk; created quick-reference guides; ran ad-hoc training sessions

4. **Stabilization (Weeks 1-4 post-go-live):**
   - Daily monitoring of key metrics: Invoice posting, cash application, customer master integrity
   - Bug fixes deployed daily; communication updates to stakeholders
   - User feedback captured; non-critical enhancements logged for Phase 2

**Phase 4: Optimization & Expansion (Months 15-36)**
1. **EMEA Optimization (Month 15-18):**
   - Process improvements enabled by new platform (e.g., automated dunning, real-time dashboards)
   - Knowledge transfer from implementation team to operations team
   - User adoption stabilized; productivity returned to baseline + 8% improvement

2. **Regions 2 & 3 Replications (Months 19-36):**
   - Leveraged EMEA playbook for APAC and Americas rollouts
   - Shortened timeline: APAC 5 months (vs. EMEA 6 months), Americas 4 months
   - Improved due to lessons learned and standardized cutover process

3. **JDE Sunset (Month 37-42):**
   - Migrated last JDE instances to S4/HANA
   - Retired legacy systems; closed data centers

**Result:**
- **System Consolidation:** 4 ECC + 2 JDE instances → 1 unified S4/HANA platform
- **Cost:** $2.5M annual maintenance → $800K (68% reduction)
- **Standardization:** 85% standardized O2C processes across regions (vs. 40% historically)
- **Performance:** Invoice processing improved 15%; cash application cycle time reduced 3 days
- **Data Quality:** Single, consolidated customer master (8,000 duplicates eliminated)
- **Capability:** Enabled advanced analytics, automated workflows, real-time reporting

**How to Communicate:**
*"I've led large-scale ERP migrations—complex undertakings involving data migration, process redesign, organizational change. Key lessons: (1) Use migration as opportunity to improve processes, not just re-implement legacy; (2) Invest heavily in data preparation—garbage in, garbage out; (3) Communicate relentlessly; (4) Plan for stabilization period post-go-live. My approach combines detailed planning with flexibility to adapt when issues emerge. The result: Successful transitions that deliver business value."*

## 4. Global O2C/AR Operations Excellence {#global-operations}

### How You Manage Complex Global Environments

**Key Principles:**

1. **Standardization = Efficiency**
   - Unified invoicing process → economies of scale in shared services
   - Standardized GL accounts → faster consolidation
   - Unified customer master → single source of truth

2. **Localization = Compliance + Customer Satisfaction**
   - Tax/regulatory variations by country
   - Customer payment preferences (bank transfer, credit card, local methods)
   - Relationship-based vs. transactional customer management

3. **Technology = Enabler of Both**
   - Configurable systems (SAP) allow standardization + local flexibility
   - Automation reduces manual intervention, improves consistency
   - Analytics enable proactive management

### Case Study: Global DSO Reduction Initiative

**Situation:**
Organization with $3B AR portfolio managed across 40+ countries; DSO 34 days (vs. industry benchmark 28 days); $180M excess working capital tied up

**Approach:**

1. **Diagnostic by Region:**
   - Invoice delivery: 14 days (US) to 22 days (Europe, tax complexities)
   - Collection effectiveness: 45% (Japan, relationship-based) to 78% (US, competitive pressure)
   - Payment terms: Net 30 to Net 60+ (industry norms vary)

2. **Quick Wins (Month 1-2):**
   - Early payment discount optimization: Analyzed which customers had discount eligibility; promoted heavily → $8M annual cash flow benefit
   - Invoice delivery acceleration: 22 days → 16 days (improved SAP processing, earlier invoice transmission)
   - Collections efficiency: Improved dunning rules; reduced manual follow-up calls by 30%

3. **Medium-term Initiatives (Month 3-6):**
   - Customer master enrichment: Captured payment behavior data, relationship importance, credit score
   - Segmented collection strategy: High-value customers (relationship-based) vs. transactional (automated dunning)
   - Negotiated payment terms with top 100 customers: "Can we move from Net 60 to Net 45?" → achieved with 25%
   - Automated cash application: Reduced suspension rate from 8% to 1%

4. **Transformation (Month 6-12):**
   - Invested in Autobank and analytics platform
   - Deployed predictive DSO model: Identify customers at risk of late payment
   - Integrated with credit team: Credit decisions informed by payment history
   - Treasury coordination: Real-time cash position dashboard; daily communications on expected receipts

**Result:**
- **DSO:** 34 days → 27 days (-7 days, -21%)
- **Cash Impact:** $180M → $220M working capital release (one-time); $45M carry-forward annual benefit
- **Collections:** Improvement in on-time payment by 18%
- **Customer Impact:** No deterioration in relationships; some customers appreciated early payment discount opportunity

**How to Position:**
*"I manage complex global O2C environments with multiple currencies, tax regimes, and customer preferences. My focus is always on cash optimization while maintaining customer relationships and compliance. In this role, I'd bring that same discipline—balancing global standardization for efficiency with local flexibility for compliance and customer management."*

## 5. Outsourcing Partner Management & Stabilization {#outsourcing-management}

### How to Manage Multiple BPO Partners Effectively

**Governance Framework:**

**1. SLA Definition & Monitoring**
- Clear metrics: Invoice posting time, exception rate, SLA compliance %
- Tiered performance: Green (on-target), Yellow (at-risk), Red (failure)
- Consequences: Bonus for exceeding, penalty for missing

**2. Regular Business Reviews**
- Monthly Operational Reviews (MOR): Transaction metrics, issues, escalations
- Quarterly Business Reviews (QBR): Trend analysis, capability gaps, opportunities
- Annual Strategic Reviews: Partnership roadmap, scope expansion, capability investments

**3. Communication Protocol**
- Escalation matrix: Operational issues → escalate to manager; strategic issues → escalate to VP
- Transparency: Share business context; help partner understand their role in company success
- Feedback: Regular conversations about what's working and what needs improvement

**4. Incentive Alignment**
- Performance bonus: "Achieve 96% SLA compliance, <2% exception rate → earn 2% revenue bonus"
- Penalty: "Fall below thresholds → 2% revenue reduction"
- Share wins: "Successfully processed 1M transactions; reduced exceptions 40% → let's celebrate together"

### Real Example: Managing Multiple Partners During Crisis

**Situation:**
During COVID-19, two of three BPO partners faced lockdowns; business continuity plan was inadequate; risk of service disruption on $2B AR portfolio

**Response:**

1. **Immediate Actions:**
   - Called each partner CEO directly: "How can we help? What do you need from us?"
   - Identified critical staff; coordinated with HR to ensure continuity
   - Increased communication frequency: Daily calls (vs. weekly)

2. **Contingency Activation:**
   - Redistributed workload: Moved highest-risk transactions to partner with least disruption
   - Activated backup offshore locations (partner had offices in 3 countries; shifted work geographically)
   - Brought onshore some time-sensitive work (billing cycles, major customer invoices)

3. **Support:**
   - Provided COVID-19 guidance from our corporate guidelines
   - Offered IT support for remote work enablement
   - Flexible timeline: "We know you're managing staff health; we'll adjust SLA targets for 90 days while you stabilize"

4. **Outcome:**
   - Prevented business disruption
   - Actual service level: 94% vs. normal 97% (but acceptable given circumstances)
   - Partner loyalty increased: "We felt you were partners, not just demanding a vendor"
   - Post-COVID, partnership strengthened; expanded scope to Collections

**How to Position:**
*"I've managed complex outsourcing relationships across multiple partners. The key is treating them as partners, not just vendors—sharing business context, providing support during challenges, and recognizing exceptional performance. I'm experienced in SLA management, performance monitoring, and escalation governance. I'm also comfortable with difficult conversations when partners miss commitments, but always within a framework of mutual success."*

## 9. Financial Data Accuracy & Controls {#financial-accuracy}

### How You Ensure AR/O2C Data Accuracy in Shared Services

**Control Framework:**

**1. Data Governance**
- **Master Data:** Customer master, GL accounts, dunning texts → centralized, version-controlled
- **Real-time Validation:** Invoice posting validates customer exists, GL code is valid, amount is reasonable
- **Reconciliation:** Monthly GL ↔ subledger reconciliation; quarterly audit reconciliation

**2. Three-Way Matching (O2C)**
- Invoice ↔ PO ↔ Receipt Match
- Variance thresholds: 2% tolerance (configurable)
- Exception escalation: Mismatches require management approval

**3. Cash Application Controls**
- Automated matching with human review for exceptions
- Bank reconciliation: Daily (not monthly)
- Suspense account: Cleaned up same-day

**4. Collections & Credit Controls**
- Credit decision approval workflow: Approvals based on amount/customer tier
- Collections escalation: Automatic dunning sequences
- Credit hold enforcement: System prevents invoicing to customers exceeding credit limit

**5. Audit Trail & Compliance**
- Complete transaction history: Source, approvals, modifications, posting date
- Regulatory compliance: Tax withholding, 1099 reporting, audit trail for financial statements
- Monthly audit support: AR agings, cutoff procedures, revenue recognition review

**6. KPI Dashboard & Monitoring**
- Daily monitoring: Invoice posting count, exception rate, cash application aging
- Weekly review: DSO, collection effectiveness, AR aging
- Monthly: AR aging by business unit, audit adjustments, reserve adequacy

## 12. Closing Questions & Strategy {#closing-questions}

### Questions to Ask at the END of the Interview (These Demonstrate Your Level)

#### For SENIOR MANAGER Role:

1. **"Can you describe the typical monthly/quarterly close process? Where are the main bottlenecks today?"**
   - *Why:* Shows you're thinking about operational integration, process improvement
   - *Listen for:* Close timeline, key dependencies, pain points

2. **"What does the outsourcing partner relationship look like? How mature is that partnership?"**
   - *Why:* You'll likely partner closely; want to understand baseline
   - *Listen for:* SLA compliance, communication frequency, capability maturity

3. **"What are the top 2-3 process improvement priorities you'd expect the new leader to focus on in the first 100 days?"**
   - *Why:* Gets specific on expectations
   - *Listen for:* Quick wins vs. long-term; alignment with your expertise

4. **"How much autonomy does the Senior Manager have in decision-making vs. escalation to the Director?"**
   - *Why:* Understand decision rights and empowerment
   - *Listen for:* Supportive leadership; clarity on scope

#### For DIRECTOR Role:

1. **"Can you walk me through the current O2C/AR operating model—how is it structured? How is it performing?"**
   - *Why:* Shows you're thinking strategically about design
   - *Listen for:* Complexity, performance gaps, transformation opportunities

2. **"What's the role's relationship to the CFO? How often do we interact? What are CFO's main priorities for O2C/AR?"**
   - *Why:* Want to understand executive partnership and priorities
   - *Listen for:* Strategic vs. tactical focus; executive engagement level

3. **"Tell me about your outsourcing strategy. Are there plans to expand, consolidate, or change partners?"**
   - *Why:* Shows you think about partnership governance and strategy
   - *Listen for:* Satisfaction, strategic direction, capability gaps

4. **"What does success look like in this role in Year 1? Year 3?"**
   - *Why:* Understand expectations and career trajectory
   - *Listen for:* Transformation goals, business impact, organizational health

5. **"Who are my key peer stakeholders—CFO, CIO, Chief Procurement, Treasurer? How aligned is the team?"**
   - *Why:* Understand organizational dynamics and collaboration landscape
   - *Listen for:* Team health, alignment, potential friction points

### Powerful Closing Statement (Applies to Both Levels):

*"Thank you for the thorough conversation. I'm excited about this opportunity. Based on our discussion, here's how I'd approach the first 100 days:*

*First 30 days: Deep dive into current state—meet the team, understand operations, review metrics, identify quick wins.*

*Next 30 days: Develop a 90-day improvement plan in partnership with stakeholders—process optimization, automation opportunities, organizational alignment.*

*By end of 90 days: Deliver early wins (DSO reduction, cost savings, efficiency gains) to build momentum and credibility.*

*I'm drawn to this organization because [specific reason: transformation opportunity, strategic mission, culture]. I bring 20+ years of O2C/AR experience, proven track record of leading complex transformations, and a passion for building high-performing teams. I'd love to contribute to [company]'s success and grow in this role."*

Before the interview:
- [ ] Research company's financial reports—understand their AR, revenue, cash flow metrics

- [ ] Quantify your results; use data to support claims
- [ ] Connect your experience to their specific challenges

- [ ] Show respect for interviewers' expertise
- [ ] Ask substantive questions at the end

After the interview:
- [ ] Send thank-you email within 24 hours; reference specific discussion points
- [ ] Reiterate interest and how you'd add value
- [ ] Ask about timeline and next steps
